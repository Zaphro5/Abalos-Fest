Discover the Festivals of Camarines Sur

Welcome to Camarines Sur

Camarines Sur, located in the Bicol Region of the Philippines, is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and colorful festivals. The province is home to some of the most exciting celebrations in the country, blending faith, tradition, and community spirit. Join us as we explore the biggest and most beloved festivals of Camarines Sur.

Tinagba Festival

History/Origin: The Tinagba Festival is an ancient agricultural tradition celebrated in Iriga City, rooted in the Bicolano custom of offering the first harvest to Bathala (the Supreme God) as a sign of gratitude. This practice dates back to pre-colonial times, when indigenous tribes performed rituals to ensure a bountiful harvest. When Christianity spread in the region, the tradition merged with Catholic beliefs and became part of the local culture. Today, the festival features colorful parades of decorated carabao-drawn carts filled with fruits and vegetables, which are later distributed to the poor. It serves as a reminder of the Bicolanosâ€™ generosity, resilience, and deep connection to the land.

Date: February 11

Activities: Float parade, cultural performances, and ritual offerings.

Boa-Boahan Festival

History/Origin: The Boa-Boahan Festival is a pre-colonial ritualistic celebration held in Nabua, Camarines Sur, to honor the ancient Bicolano ancestors known as the Boa people. According to folklore, early Bicolanos performed rice-throwing rituals to appease the gods and ensure prosperity for the coming year. This practice was later incorporated into the Christian tradition, making it a unique fusion of indigenous and Catholic beliefs. The festival is highlighted by reenactments of the old rituals, street dances, and colorful performances that depict the rich history of Nabua. It is a symbol of the townâ€™s deep cultural heritage and its peopleâ€™s enduring respect for their ancestors.

Date: Every April

Activities: Ritual dances, coconut tossing ceremony, and colorful parades.

Kamundagan Festival

History/Origin: The Kamundagan Festival is a month-long Christmas celebration in Naga City, which signifies the rebirth of hope, peace, and unity among its people. The term "Kamundagan" means "birth" in the local dialect, reflecting the joyous spirit of Christmas. The festival started as a way to bring communities together through festive events and charitable activities. One of the main highlights is the grand Christmas tree lighting ceremony, symbolizing the light of faith and goodwill. Over time, the festival has grown to include lantern parades, cultural shows, fireworks displays, and gift-giving activities, making it a cherished holiday tradition in Camarines Sur.

Date: December (Whole Month)

Activities: Grand Christmas tree lighting, lantern parades, street performances, and fireworks displays.
